**⚙️ The Mechanism:** The Iran conflict is hammering energy and fertilizer prices, which means farmers are about to get squeezed. Shortages of critical inputs like fuel and fertilizer could drag harvests down, just in time for the next global food crisis.

> **📊 The Analysis:** Alright, let's map this out using a good old Cobb-Douglas production function: $$Y = A K^{\alpha} L^{1-\alpha}$$. Where $Y$ is agricultural output, $A$ is technology, $K$ is capital (like fuel and fertilizer), and $L$ is labor. Now, when you jack up the price of $K$, BOTH the demand and supply curves shift in - meaning farmers produce LESS at any given price. CLASSIC deadweight loss. And with food prices already sky-high, we're about to see even more empty shelves, longer breadlines, and more #FortniteChallenge videos than anyone ever wanted.

**📈 Market Call:** Protect your land, hoard your seeds, and start learning to live off the grid, folks. We're about to feel some serious Malthusian vibes.
